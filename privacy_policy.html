3. Data collected/processed by collector/processor

3.1. Websites and infrastructure

For obvious reasons everything intended by user or bot to be stored on the platform like comment under post can be stored on the server and if that was the intention — presented on the site indefinitely. In case users wants some content they uploaded to be removed they shall contact the owner listed above.

3.1.1. Diagnostics data

The underlying infrastructure stores standard diagnostics data about connections and requests made. They include IP addresses, User-agents and other metadata sent by your web browser. This data is needed for stability and security of the platform, which is necessary.

3.1.2. Data location

While the main server is located in the European Union, some services used to deliver and back up websites are hosted in the United States. All of them however are GDPR compliant.

3.2. Traffic analytics - plausible.io

Domains listed above use plausible.io traffic analytics platform. Plausible Analytics collects only aggregated information, which does not allow us to identify any visitor to our website.
Full details of what's collected is available here: https://plausible.io/privacy.

3.3. Content Delivery Network - Cloudflare

Domains listed above use Cloudflare Content Delivery Network. This means all traffic goes also via their servers and some content including scripts and cookies may be injected. This service is required for platform stability and security, which is necessary.
Privacy policy can be accessed at https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/ - sections that apply to end user.

3.3.1. Cookies used

  • __cfduid - necessary; to identify trusted web traffic

3.4. Static content - Google Fonts and gstatic.com

Some parts of websites listed above unfortunately still utilise Google Fonts and other assets from gstatic.com. Fonts and other visual assets are necessary for correct website rendering.
Google has no specific privacy policy related to those services, but a general one is available at https://policies.google.com/privacy.

4. What to do if you don't want your data being processed

You can disable cookies in your browser (example tutorial: https://www.wikihow.com/Disable-Cookies) and disable JavaScript using web browser extensions like NoScript (https://noscript.net/). You can change your User-agent as well (example tutorial: https://www.howtogeek.com/113439/how-to-change-your-browsers-user-agent-without-installing-any-extensions/).

5. Changes

Current privacy policy version is always maintained at https://skowron.ski/privacy_policy.html.

5.1. Expected changes

  • It is expected that diagnostics data described under (3.1.1) will be stripped of IP addresses and detailed data sent in User-agent field.
  • It is expected that static content served by Google Fonts and gstatic.com described under (4) will be moved to servers hosting websites themselves.
